Typical Dishes of Concepción

Empanada de Mariscos

Traditional seafood empanada, stuffed with fresh shellfish from the coast of Concepción.

Chorrillana

A hearty dish with fries, meat, onions, and fried eggs — a local favorite for sharing.

Curanto

A traditional southern Chilean meal of shellfish, meat, and potatoes cooked underground.

Cazuela

A comforting soup with chicken or beef, corn, pumpkin, and vegetables.

Paila Marina

A traditional Chilean seafood stew or soup, often served in a clay bowl ("paila"). It typically features a mix of various shellfish (like mussels, clams, and shrimp) and fish simmered in a flavorful broth made with fish stock, vegetables like onion and garlic, and herbs and spices like paprika and parsley.

Humitas

Fresh ground corn, seasoned and wrapped in corn husks, steamed to perfection. Humitas are similar in concept to tamales, but differ in that they are made from fresh corn, not masa.

Pastel de Choclo

Pastel de choclo (choclo pie) is a dish that originated in the colonial era, corresponding to a mestizo dish that combines Spanish pine with Chilean corn paste, and was created by Mapuche cooks in the kitchens of the Spanish settlers.

Pajaritos

is a traditional Chilean sweet made of soft, anise-flavored bread rolls covered in a meringue glaze, often decorated with colorful sprinkles. In English, these are often called sweet little birds or sweet birdies because their shape resembles a small bird. They are a popular treat, especially for afternoon tea, called once in Chile.
